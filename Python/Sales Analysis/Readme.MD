## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) of sales data collected during the Diwali festival season in India. The primary goal is to uncover meaningful patterns in customer purchasing behaviour across various demographics, geographies, and product categories — enabling data-driven decisions for targeted marketing and inventory planning.

The dataset comprises **11,251 records** and **15 features**, including customer demographics (age, gender, marital status), regional data (state, zone), occupation, product category, and purchase amounts.

Key objectives of this analysis include:

- Understanding which customer segments (age group, gender, occupation) drive the most revenue
- Identifying top-performing states and zones during the festive season
- Analysing product category preferences across different demographics
- Deriving actionable insights to improve sales strategy for future Diwali campaigns

## 📊 Key Insights & Findings

### 👩 Gender
- Female customers significantly outnumber male customers (~7,800 vs ~3,400), and also generate higher total purchase amounts, making women the primary consumer segment during Diwali.

### 🎂 Age Group
- The **26–35 age group** dominates purchases across both genders, with females in this group being the single largest buying segment (3,269 orders). This is followed by the 36–45 and 18–25 groups, indicating that young-to-mid-aged adults are the core target audience.

### 💼 Occupation
- **IT Sector** (1,583) and **Healthcare** (1,408) professionals are the most active buyers, followed by **Aviation** (1,310) and **Banking** (1,137). These high-income occupations represent the most valuable customer segments.

### 🛍️ Product Category
- **Clothing & Apparel** (2,655) and **Food** (2,490) are the top-selling categories by order count, followed by **Electronics & Gadgets** (2,087) and **Footwear & Shoes** (1,059). This suggests that festive gifting and personal consumption drive the most sales during Diwali.
